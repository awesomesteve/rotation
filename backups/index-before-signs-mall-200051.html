Settings
When enabled, real clouds, rain, and lightning from the current weather show on the skyline.
Photo backgrounds will appear here once you drop the image files into the app folder. See instructions below.
Generates a code with your roster, schedules, dates & tags (no photos). Send it over WhatsApp. Friend pastes it into the same screen on their app to merge in your data.
Export saves everything (profiles, photos, schedules, dates, tags) to a small file on your phone. "Save to Google Drive" opens the share sheet — pick Drive from the list. To restore later, tap Import.
To add a song: drop the .mp3 into the audio/ folder, then add one line to audio/songs.json — it will appear here automatically. Fades out after 25 seconds.
A PIN screen appears when you open the app. Locks the roster from prying eyes if someone picks up your phone.
How to use real photo backgrounds:
Save each photo into the same folder as index.html using these exact names (PNG or JPG both work):
• bg-day.png or bg-day.jpg — daytime skyline
• bg-night.png or bg-night.jpg — night skyline
• bg-sunset.png or bg-sunset.jpg — sunset skyline
Then reload the app — the option becomes active automatically.

🚶 Daygame locations
Places to bump into Jakartans during the day — university-age and working-age. Be respectful, read the room, leave gracefully if the vibe isn't there.
🎓 Universities (public-access campuses)
📍Universitas Indonesia (UI) — Depok Huge wooded campus, lake, central library, cafés along the perimeter. Open to public. 📍Binus University — Anggrek & Alam Sutera Big international student crowd, English-friendly. Lots of cafés right outside. 📍Atma Jaya — Semanggi Central Sudirman location, mall-adjacent. Working-age crowd mixes in at lunch. 📍Prasetiya Mulya — BSD & Cilandak Business school, well-dressed crowd, lots of group projects at nearby cafés. 📍Trisakti — Grogol West Jakarta, large student population, busy café strip on the perimeter.
☕ Hipster & laptop-friendly cafés
☕Kopi Tuku — Cipete & everywhere Local-chain icon, packed with 20-somethings, easy small-talk opener. ☕Tanamera Coffee — multiple branches Specialty coffee, working professionals, slower turnover = more time to talk. ☕Common Grounds — Sudirman / Citywalk Bright, photogenic, laptop city. Always busy weekday lunchtimes. ☕Anomali Coffee — Senopati Senopati strip is THE young-professional after-work zone. ☕ST. ALi — Setiabudi Aussie-style brunch spot, laptop-friendly, English-speaking crowd. ☕Filosofi Kopi — Melawai From the movie. Touristy but locals love it. Mixed crowd.
🛍 Daytime shopping miles & mall strips
🛍Plaza Indonesia — Bundaran HI Upmarket, professional women on lunch break. Best around 12-2pm. 🛍Grand Indonesia Massive, varied crowd. East Mall is younger; West Mall is more professional. 🛍Senayan City + Plaza Senayan Sudirman office crowd. Strong working-age weekday traffic. 🛍Pacific Place — SCBD Finance & consultancy crowd, very dressed-up. 🛍Pondok Indah Mall (PIM) Old-money South Jakarta, established families, polished crowd.
🍻 After-work hangouts
🍷Senopati strip The single best 5-9pm zone in Jakarta. Walkable bar-crawl strip. 🍷Kemang Expat-heavy, more relaxed than Senopati, lots of outdoor bars. 🍷SCBD (Sudirman Central Business District) Office tower happy hours. Best Thursdays/Fridays from 6pm. 🎶M Bloc Space — Blok M Live music + food court vibes, very dateable. 🍷Sarinah Thamrin Recently revamped, rooftop bars, mixed crowd, weekend lively.
🏋 Fitness & healthy lifestyle
🏃Senayan jogging track + GBK Sunday morning Car Free Day is a huge social scene. 🌳Hutan Kota by Plataran — GBK Outdoor café surrounded by greenery. Brunch crowd. 🌳Taman Suropati — Menteng Sunday acoustic jams, students with instruments. Easy approach territory.
